3.0 out of 5 stars143. Easy peasy sleazy Breezy
By Scarlet Jupiter TOP 1000 REVIEWER on December 20, 2015
Format: Audio CD Verified Purchase
Recent social media posts, and naming this new project after his daughter, suggest that Chris Brown is maturing, and focuses more on changing his life for the better. Just like the beautiful, atmospheric cover art for this, of a serene grown man sharing an intimate moment with his daughter. Thus, it is ironic that this very record is his most sexually explicit so far. Those who are offended by profane language, make sure you buy the clean version of this, otherwise you will find yourselves cringing with embarassment by the straight-forward sexual content that runs this project, in complete contrast with the album's name. "Fine by me", "Zero", and "No filter" are some of the good tracks on here; CB is at his best when playful and fun. "Blow in the wind" finds him nonchalant addressing the haters, while "Wrist" presents his edgier side rather successfully. "KAE" and the soulful "Make love" are some of the rare heartfelt moments, the best of which is "Little more (Royalty)", the only song that talks directly about his daughter, and easily one of the best tracks on here. With quite a few weak tracks, and the vulgar nature of most songs, those who expect a more tender and deeper Chris Brown than before, will have to wait until he shows more honesty, with less sex, partying, greed and misogyny in his songs. He still is quite far from being the best man and artist that he wants to be. Which is a shame as "Royalty" could have been a king-like album.

5.0 out of 5 starsDon't judge ROYALTY by its cover
By Petar Stajic on February 27, 2016
Format: Audio CD
I resisted this album for awhile - mostly 'cos of the album cover ... But I have it now for a couple of weeks and I can't stop playing it. I also got a few "mix-tapes" from Chris Brown that leaked around the same time ROYALTY was released, including 5-track EP from iTunes ROYALTY INTERNATIONAL EP. Chris clearly had great selection of tracks to choose from to put this album together. All songs are great - my hat's off to Chris for arguably his best effort yet. He is clearly HAPPY, inspired and creatively on the TOP of the game ... There are about 5 or 6 songs on R'n'B / hip-hop mainstream radio right now, from another artists that have Chris Brown as "featured artist" and every song is GOOD to great, and that's only because Chris is on it.

I never NOT liked something from Chris. I own his complete solo catalogue and I saw him perform once. I guess I am a fan. He is multi talented, his videos are very entertaining and his voice is absolutely amazing. Hopefully he will continue to focus on the music and his talent, and that will over-ride media circus about his personal life. ROYALTY is surprisingly great album from Chris Brown and it seems like it is getting attention from fans that this album deserves. The collection on the disc include 14 tracks that go from slow to up-beat tempo throughout the whole experience. ROYALTY over all feels like 80-ies tribute, in so many ways. There's some Whitney, Chaka, George Michael in the mix, as far 80-ies that I can recognise - Chris does it really well. It's very positive sounding and little bit too explicit, which makes it definitely NOT 80-es.
